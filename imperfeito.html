The Imperfect Tense.

The Imperfect tense is used to describe an action that was ongoing in the past, i.e. an action that used to happen for an extended period of time, or an action that happened at the same time as another action.

Regular verbs are conjugated accordingly in the imperfect tense:



-ar verbs -er and -ir verbs
-ava -ávamos -ia -íamos
-avas -áveis -ias -íeis
-ava -avam -ia -iam
